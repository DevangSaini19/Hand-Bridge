# Complete ISL/ASL Gesture Vocabulary

This document lists all sign language gestures supported by the Bridge Hands translation system. The system now recognizes **60+ gestures** including full A-Z alphabet, numbers 0-10, and common sign language phrases.

- **Alphabet**: 26 letters (A-Z)

- **Common Gestures**: 8 gestures (Bye, Call Me, Dislike, Good Job, Good Luck, Peace, Praying, Rock On)
- **Words & Phrases**: 20+ common signs
- **Total**: 60+ unique gestures

## 🔢 Numbers (0-10)

### 0 - Zero
- **Hand Shape**: Closed fist
- **Description**: All fingers curled into palm, thumb wrapped around fingers
- **Alternative Names**: Finished, Stop

### 1 - One
- **Hand Shape**: Index finger extended
- **Description**: Only index finger pointing up, all other fingers closed

### 2 - Two
- **Hand Shape**: Index and middle fingers together
- **Description**: Index and middle fingers extended close together, other fingers closed

### 3 - Three
- **Hand Shape**: Index, middle, and ring fingers extended
- **Description**: Three fingers pointing up, thumb and pinky closed

### 4 - Four
- **Hand Shape**: Four fingers extended
- **Description**: Index, middle, ring, and pinky extended, thumb tucked

### 5 - Five
- **Hand Shape**: All fingers spread open
- **Description**: All five fingers extended and spread apart

### 6 - Six
- **Hand Shape**: Thumb and pinky extended
- **Description**: Thumb and pinky extended outward (Y handshape)

### 7 - Seven
- **Hand Shape**: Thumb, index, middle, and ring fingers extended
- **Description**: Four fingers extended, pinky closed

### 8 - Eight
- **Hand Shape**: Thumb and index forming C
- **Description**: Thumb and index finger forming a C shape

### 9 - Nine
- **Hand Shape**: Three fingers with thumb
- **Description**: Thumb, index, and middle fingers extended

### 10 - Ten
- **Hand Shape**: Two hands required
- **Description**: Both hands with fingers extended

## 🔤 Alphabet (A-Z)

### A
- **Hand Shape**: Closed fist with thumb to the side
- **Description**: Fist with thumb alongside fingers

### B
- **Hand Shape**: Four fingers extended
- **Description**: Same as number 4 - flat hand with thumb tucked

### C
- **Hand Shape**: Curved fingers
- **Description**: Fingers curved to form C shape

### D
- **Hand Shape**: Index up with thumb touching other fingers
- **Description**: Index finger pointing up, thumb touches middle finger forming circle

### E
- **Hand Shape**: Fingers curled, thumb tucked
- **Description**: All fingers curled tightly

### F
- **Hand Shape**: OK sign
- **Description**: Thumb and index forming circle, other fingers extended

### G
- **Hand Shape**: Index and thumb horizontal
- **Description**: Index and thumb extended horizontally

### H
- **Hand Shape**: Index and middle horizontal
- **Description**: Index and middle fingers extended horizontally together

### I
- **Hand Shape**: Pinky extended only
- **Description**: Only pinky finger extended upward

### J
- **Hand Shape**: Pinky extended with motion
- **Description**: Pinky extended, traced in J shape (motion required)

### K
- **Hand Shape**: Index and middle up with thumb between
- **Description**: Index and middle fingers up, thumb touches middle finger base

### L
- **Hand Shape**: Thumb and index forming L
- **Description**: Thumb extended horizontally, index pointing up forming L shape

### M
- **Hand Shape**: Three fingers over thumb
- **Description**: Thumb under first three fingers

### N
- **Hand Shape**: Two fingers over thumb
- **Description**: Thumb under index and middle fingers

### O
- **Hand Shape**: Fingers forming circle
- **Description**: All fingers curved forming O shape

### P
- **Hand Shape**: Index and middle down, thumb extended
- **Description**: Index and middle pointing down, thumb extended

### Q
- **Hand Shape**: Index and thumb pointing down
- **Description**: Index finger and thumb extended downward

### R
- **Hand Shape**: Index and middle crossed
- **Description**: Index and middle fingers crossed together

### S
- **Hand Shape**: Fist with thumb across fingers
- **Description**: Closed fist with thumb wrapped across front

### T
- **Hand Shape**: Thumb between index and middle
- **Description**: Thumb tucked between index and middle fingers

### U
- **Hand Shape**: Index and middle together pointing up
- **Description**: Index and middle fingers together, extended upward

### V
- **Hand Shape**: Index and middle apart
- **Description**: Index and middle fingers spread apart in V shape

### W
- **Hand Shape**: Three fingers up
- **Description**: Index, middle, and ring fingers extended

### X
- **Hand Shape**: Index bent
- **Description**: Index finger bent at middle joint forming hook

### Y
- **Hand Shape**: Thumb and pinky extended
- **Description**: Thumb and pinky extended outward (same as number 6)

### Z
- **Hand Shape**: Index traces Z
- **Description**: Index finger traces letter Z in air (motion required)

## 🤝 Common Gestures (From Repository)

### 1. Bye
- **Hand Shape**: Open palm waving
- **Description**: All five fingers extended, palm facing forward
- **Usage**: Farewell, goodbye
- **Complexity**: Moderate

### 2. Call Me
- **Hand Shape**: Y handshape (thumb and pinky)
- **Description**: Thumb and pinky extended, held to ear
- **Usage**: "Phone me" or "call me later"
- **Complexity**: Moderate

### 3. Dislike
- **Hand Shape**: Thumbs down
- **Description**: Closed fist with thumb pointing downward
- **Usage**: Disapproval, disagreement, dislike
- **Complexity**: Simple

### 4. Good Job
- **Hand Shape**: Thumbs up
- **Description**: Closed fist with thumb pointing upward
- **Usage**: Approval, agreement, well done
- **Complexity**: Simple

### 5. Good Luck
- **Hand Shape**: Crossed fingers (R handshape)
- **Description**: Index and middle fingers crossed
- **Usage**: Wishing good fortune, hoping for best
- **Complexity**: Moderate

### 6. Peace
- **Hand Shape**: V sign with spread fingers
- **Description**: Index and middle fingers spread apart in V shape
- **Usage**: Peace sign, victory
- **Complexity**: Simple

### 7. Praying
- **Hand Shape**: Two hands together
- **Description**: Palms pressed together in prayer position
- **Usage**: Prayer, gratitude, Namaste
- **Complexity**: Complex (requires two hands)

### 8. Rock On
- **Hand Shape**: Index and pinky extended with thumb
- **Description**: Index finger and pinky extended, thumb out
- **Usage**: Rock and roll sign, love sign
- **Complexity**: Moderate

## 💬 Common Words & Phrases

### Greetings
- **Hello**: Open palm, all fingers extended
- **Goodbye**: Same as "Bye" gesture
- **Welcome**: Open hands moving toward body

### Courtesy
- **Please**: Circular motion on chest (motion required)
- **Thank You**: Fingers to chin moving forward (or praying hands)
- **Sorry**: Fist on chest with circular motion (motion required)
- **Excuse Me**: Open hand moving across body

### Affirmations
- **Yes**: Fist nodding motion (S handshape)
- **No**: Index and middle fingers tapping thumb
- **OK**: Thumb and index forming circle (F handshape)
- **Good**: Thumbs up
- **Bad**: Thumbs down
- **Maybe**: Hands alternating up and down

### Actions
- **Stop**: Palm facing forward, all fingers up
- **Wait**: Same as Stop
- **Go**: Pointing motion
- **Come**: Hand beckoning toward body
- **Help**: Fist on open palm lifting up

## 🎯 Recognition Accuracy

The system uses the following confidence thresholds:

- **High Confidence** (85-95%): Simple gestures like numbers 1-5, letters A, B, I, V
- **Medium Confidence** (75-85%): Most alphabet letters, common gestures
- **Lower Confidence** (65-75%): Complex gestures requiring precise finger positions

### Tips for Better Recognition:

1. **Lighting**: Ensure good, even lighting on your hands
2. **Background**: Use a plain, contrasting background
3. **Hand Position**: Keep hands centered in camera view
4. **Distance**: Maintain 1-2 feet from camera
5. **Stability**: Hold gestures steady for 0.5-1 second
6. **Practice**: Some gestures require practice for precision

## 📝 Technical Implementation

### Gesture Categories

The system categorizes gestures into 5 types:

1. **number**: Numerical signs (0-10)
2. **alphabet**: Letter signs (A-Z)
3. **word**: Single word signs (Hello, Good, Bad, etc.)
4. **phrase**: Multi-word phrases (Thank You, Good Luck, etc.)
5. **action**: Action-based signs (Stop, Go, Help, etc.)

### Complexity Levels

- **Simple**: Single hand, basic finger positions
- **Moderate**: Single hand, precise finger positioning required
- **Complex**: Two hands required or motion tracking needed

### Hand Features Used

The recognition system analyzes:

- **Fingers Extended**: Which fingers are up/down
- **Palm Orientation**: Direction palm is facing (up, down, left, right, forward, back)
- **Hand Openness**: Degree of finger spread (0.0 to 1.0)
- **Thumb Position**: Specific thumb placement (up, down, side, crossed)
- **Finger Distances**: Spacing between specific fingertips
- **Hand Count**: Single hand vs two hands

## 🛠️ For Developers

### Adding New Gestures

To add a new gesture to the system:

1. **Analyze Hand Shape**: Determine which fingers are extended
2. **Measure Features**: Calculate palm orientation, hand openness, etc.
3. **Add Recognition Logic**: Insert condition in `recognizeAdvancedGesture()` method
4. **Set Category**: Assign appropriate category (number, alphabet, word, phrase, action)
5. **Define Confidence**: Set confidence threshold (0.65-0.95)
6. **Add Translation**: Update `translationEngine.ts` with translations
7. **Test**: Verify recognition accuracy with real camera input

### Code Structure

```typescript
// Priority-based recognition system:
1. Closed fist gestures (0, A, E, S)
2. One finger gestures (1, I, thumbs up/down)
3. Two finger gestures (2, V, L, Y, Peace, Call Me)
4. Three finger gestures (3, W, K, D)
5. Four finger gestures (4, B, P, C, O)
6. Five finger gestures (5, F, Hello, Stop)
7. Two hand gestures (10, Praying, Thank You)
8. Additional alphabet letters (M, N, T, X)
```
